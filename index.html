You Have Questions. We Have Data.

Enclomiphene is a selective estrogen receptor modulator (SERM) that works at the pituitary gland. Unlike traditional testosterone replacement therapy, which introduces external testosterone and may shut down your body's natural production, enclomiphene blocks estrogen feedback at the pituitary. This prompts your body to increase its own LH and FSH production, which in turn signals natural testosterone production. The result is that your body produces its own testosterone rather than relying on external sources. All treatment decisions are made by a licensed physician based on your individual health profile.
One of the key advantages of enclomiphene over traditional TRT is its potential to preserve fertility. Traditional testosterone therapy may significantly reduce sperm production because external testosterone signals the brain to reduce LH and FSH — the hormones that drive sperm production. Enclomiphene, by contrast, works by increasing LH and FSH, which may support both testosterone production and spermatogenesis. Your physician will discuss fertility considerations during your consultation.
